    Where the day's electricity comes from

    Assumptions, sources & the math — every figure, where it comes from, and exactly how it's combined

    The whole model is a one-day, hour-by-hour balance of supply against demand, run for 24 hours. Every amber number below is a sourced constant from the table further down; everything else is your slider input or arithmetic. Nothing is hidden.

    1 · Demand each hour (GW)

    demand[h] = Peak × shape[h] — Peak = your demand slider (256 GW today → 459 by 2036)
    shape[h] = 24-hour curve, average ≈ 0.80 of peak, topping out at 18:00
    annual energy (BU/yr) = Σ demand[h] × 365 ÷ 1000

    2 · How much each source can supply that hour (GW)

    solar[h] = Solar_GW × 0.55 × max(0, sin(π·(h−6)/12)) 6am–6pm, else 0 → fleet CUF ≈ 17.5%
    wind[h] = Wind_GW × profile[h] evening-weighted curve → fleet CUF ≈ 22%
    nuclear = 0.80 × Nuclear_GW flat, runs all day
    coal cap = 0.85 × Coal_GW   gas cap = 0.85 × Gas_GW
    hydro: daily energy = Hydro_GW × 0.33 × 24, poured onto the highest-demand hours (≤ Hydro_GW/hr)
    battery: power = Storage_GWh ÷ 4   round-trip efficiency 0.85, starts ~70% charged

    3 · Dispatch — who fills demand each hour, and in what order

    1. Take all available solar + wind first (free, zero-carbon).
    2. Then nuclear, then hydro (firm, zero-carbon).
    3. Any leftover solar/wind charges the battery (spare beyond that is curtailed/wasted).
    4. Then coal, then gas ramp up to cover what's still missing.
    5. Finally the battery discharges to shave the evening peak.
    6. Anything still unmet = shortfall — a blackout, the red hatched area.

    4 · Turning the day into the headline numbers

    energy_source = Σ over 24h of that source's output (GWh for the day)
    Coal share % = energy_coal ÷ (all energy) × 100
    CO₂ (Mt/yr) = (energy_coal × 0.97 + energy_gas × 0.45) × 365 ÷ 1000
    Avg cost ₹/kWh = Σ(energy_source × tariff_source) ÷ (all energy)
    tariffs ₹/kWh: solar 2.6, wind 3.7, coal 4.6, gas 7.0, nuclear 4.5, hydro 4.0, storage 5.0
    Fossil imports ($bn/yr) = (coal_TWh × 0.20 × $80 + gas_TWh × 0.50 × $90) ÷ 1000
    where x_TWh = energy_x × 365 ÷ 1000; calibrates to the ~$21 bn FY24 coal-import bill

    5 · Cost to build (capacity added beyond today's fleet)

    per source: cost = max(0, fleet − today) [GW] × 1000 × capex [₹cr/MW]
    capex ₹cr/MW: coal 8.34, solar 3.5, wind 6.5, nuclear 13, hydro 10.5, gas 5 · battery 1.7 ₹cr/MWh
    + transmission: (added solar + added wind) [GW] × 1000 × 1.5 [₹cr/MW]
    ₹ lakh crore = total ₹crore ÷ 100,000    $bn = total ₹crore × 10⁷ ÷ (live ₹/USD) ÷ 10⁹

    Worked check: the CEA 2031-32 preset adds ~₹29 lakh crore by this formula — independently close to CEA's own ₹33.6 lakh crore plan figure.
